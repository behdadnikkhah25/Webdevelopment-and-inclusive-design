Reflection

Transition from high school

In this text, we will be discussing our last years in upper-secondary education in connection with the covid-19 pandemic. We have been in state of pandemic for almost two years now which has resulted in restrictions in both social interaction and education. Classes are divided into two different classrooms where there must be a distance of at least one meter. In addition, there is a reduced capacity of teachers, which leads to obstacles in teaching and placement in classrooms. How has the pandemic affected the way teachers present the subject matter, physical education at school and the social school environment?

The teaching system

The teaching has been strongly influenced by collaboration between teachers and students. This means that students must have an understanding that it can be a more independent teaching environment, where it is up to the student to take responsibility for their own education. The way we had this in our upper-secondary school was that we split the class in two. One group that had physical education at school and another group that had digital education. Such was the distribution, and it was back and forth every day where the order changes for who would have physical education and digital.

Digital challenges

The problem in this case was that there were no teachers at home with the students who had lessons digitally. The teacher also did not know in the beginning if the students are involved in the teaching, or if they are only logged in digitally. Therefore, they decided to introduce submissions and small tests to check if the pupils were paying attention in digital lessons. As a result, this led to much more homework and independent learning. There were many pupils who lost motivation since there was not as effective teaching and an increase in homework.

The exams

After a long year with a changed teaching program, the exam period was approaching. There was a discussion about whether the Directorate of Education should complete the exam, even though it had been a year of digital and physical school. The Directorate of Education believed that the exam is a golden way to assess the student's knowledge of a subject and is conducted so that the student's competence is fair and correct. This created a great deal of frustration between the students and the Directorate of Education. The students objected against what the Directorate of Education presented and argued that it was not fair to complete the exam. They justified their stance by the fact that more time was spent getting through the syllabus than has been the case before. In addition to the fact that it has varied with red and yellow levels, that is, the red zone is that there is only digital teaching, and yellow meant a combination of digital in addition to physical lessons.

Conclusion

To conclude, the previous year has been difficult and demanding for us as students. It has been an unusual year with a combination of digital and physical education. There have been many times where we have lost the motivation to work with school stuff, because there has been a lot of homework and confusion with physical and digital teaching.
