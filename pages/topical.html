Topical information

Automation

The industrial revolution brought significant changes to social conditions (Schrumpf et al., 2021). Perhaps the most important change was the workplace. Before the industrial revolution, the vast majority of production was done by individual people or groups of people who worked together (Schrumpf et al., 2021). But with the rise of cities, people moved from the countryside to work in factories. This made it possible to mass produce on a large scale and increased efficiency. In our time, automation can be likened to the industrial revolution, and like the industrial revolution, automation can bring big changes to social conditions.

The effect of automation

The development of automation can be connected to changes in societal values. The work-life balance is an important societal value whose change perhaps started long before the advent of automation (Gisle et al., 2021). Long working hours was a big social problem. Today we have laws that limit how many hours we can work in a week. Even then, if repetitive work can be automated, workers can have more time in their private lives, increase production and focus on more important tasks (Harris, 2019) (Groover, 2020). It can also lead to happier workers. An everyday example is self-service cashiers that have optimized and relieved workload. It has allowed for shorter queues, better capacity and gives more focus on customers and other tasks. Some stores hire fewer employees and instead hire higher-educated workers related to the newer technology.

Concerns

There are concerns such as the possibility that workers will become slaves to the machines and that our privacy will be endangered by the computers and data collections. Especially since automation requires large amounts of information collected from our everyday lives.

Conclusion

On the whole, automation, if used correctly, can bring great opportunities in the future. It may keep workers away from tasks that are repetitive, hazardous and may even provide greater environments socially and economically, thus letting people enjoy their lives and time more than ever.
